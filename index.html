College essays don't have to be dry. Rather than using an essay as an extra list of qualifications, we should be asking: How can I tell this story, a story that's really important to me, to a reader who doesn't know me at all? How can this essay say something real about me, and how can I make my reader feel something? The goal of a good college essay shouldn't be to mention as many extracurriculars as possible. Ideally, your reader will walk away feeling like they've gotten to know a part of you—and that they would like to get to know you a little more.
